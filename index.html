Abstract

The design of future energy systems which cope with the fluctuating availability of renewable energy and with the flexible demand of complex systems is an important societal concern. Recently, various new technologies, like power-to-heat systems and electrical vehicles, have arrived in our everyday life, making the setting even more complex. Next to reducing the overall energy consumptions, other important concerns are a higher extent of consumer flexibility so that more sustainable and locally generated energy is used, the resilience of energy supply and the efficiency of new energy systems. For all these issues, energy informatics plays a central role. It provides methods and technologies to extract and manage information from energy systems. It offers communication and information-processing principles to operate energy systems securely.

The Summer School Communication Technology and Data Analytics for Future Energy Systems focuses on informatics methods that bear a relationship with the energy sector, with a focus on telematics and data management and analytics. The summer school is a venue for graduate students, researchers and practitioners to learn about and contribute to this specific field.

The summer school is a joint event of the Gesellschaft für Informatik and the DFG Research Training Group “Energy Status Data – Informatics Methods for its Collection, Analysis and Exploitation”. Leading researchers in the field of energy informatics will give an end-to-end perspective on the fundamentals and on advanced topics of communication technology and data management and analytics.
